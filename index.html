What is Computer Vision?

Computer vision (CV) is an artificial intelligence technology that interprets and processes visual data, including images and video. In healthcare, it can support tasks such as analyzing medical images, monitoring patient activity, and evaluating procedural performance.

No prior knowledge of computer vision is required to participate in this survey.

About the Study

Understanding the Clinical Perspective

We are conducting a survey to understand how healthcare professionals view the use of computer vision in medicine. Your responses will help identify promising applications, practical challenges, and priorities for future development of AI-driven tools in healthcare.

By sharing your experience and perspective, you will contribute to a better understanding of how this technology may be used in clinical practice. This study is clinician-driven and will directly inform how CV technology is designed, evaluated, and deployed in healthcare settings worldwide.

Impact

Why Your Input Matters

"Clinical and academic stakeholders view CV as a potentially valuable tool to enhance patient care, clinician education, and documentation in diverse settings — but substantial ethical, legal, and operational concerns remain. Transparent communication and early engagement of end-users is essential for responsible integration."

This survey is part of a multi-phase research program at Mayo Clinic and Arizona State University building the evidence base for responsible CV adoption in healthcare. Findings will be published in peer-reviewed literature and shared with technology developers, hospital administrators, and policy bodies.
